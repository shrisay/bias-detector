About Us

From the design of this webpage, to the fine-tuning of the AI model behind it, this full-stack application has been built by a single student in high school. As an avid participant in Model United Nations conferences, I have spent countless hours researching numerous world issues. I came to the realization that news articles are biased more often than one might think, subtly attempting to steer their readers towards a certain ideology.

Then I wondered: what if there was a way for me to detect bias or the ever-frustrating clickbait in an article without ever reading it? Machine Learning has been a topic of my fascination for a while, and this project seemed like the perfect opportunity to learn and apply its concepts. Combining my skills from Harvard's CS50 Web, introductory ML courses on Kaggle and a little help from my older sister, I have built this webpage using Python's Django framework and JavaScript for interactive features (such as toggling dark mode).

For further information on the AI model and the working of this project, visit the How it Works section.
